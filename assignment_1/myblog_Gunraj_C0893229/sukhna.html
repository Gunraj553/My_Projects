Sukhna Lake , Established in 1958.

Sukhna Lake, nestled at the foothills of the Shivalik Range in Chandigarh, India, is a serene and picturesque man-made reservoir that captivates visitors with its natural beauty and tranquility. Created in 1958 by damming the Sukhna Choe stream, the lake covers an area of approximately 3 square kilometers, making it a prominent landmark in the city. Surrounded by lush greenery and scenic hills, Sukhna Lake offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life. The lake's pristine waters reflect the changing hues of the sky during sunrise and sunset, creating a mesmerizing ambiance that attracts photographers, nature enthusiasts, and tourists alike. A popular spot for morning and evening walks, the lake's well-maintained promenade provides a perfect setting for leisurely strolls and moments of introspection. Additionally, Sukhna Lake serves as a hub for various recreational activities such as boating, kayaking, and even yoga sessions, drawing people from all walks of life to experience its serene waters and rejuvenating atmosphere.

Apart from its natural allure, Sukhna Lake holds cultural and ecological significance for the region. The lake acts as a vital water reservoir for Chandigarh and the neighboring areas, contributing to the city's sustainable water supply. Furthermore, the Sukhna Wildlife Sanctuary, located on the lake's eastern banks, is home to a diverse range of avian species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers. With its harmonious blend of natural beauty, recreational opportunities, and ecological importance, Sukhna Lake stands as a cherished gem in the heart of Chandigarh, offering both locals and visitors a sanctuary of peace and serenity amidst the urban landscape.
